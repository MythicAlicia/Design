Typical Work Project Of An Architect

Architects design and create buildings, from homes to massive civil projects like shopping centres. It's a career that blends design talents and creativity with mathematical and engineering skills. Architects may be self-employed or work for larger architectural consultancy firms.

Architects usually work to a brief from a client, who has a vision of the kind of building they want. An architect uses specialist design software and manual sketches to create ideas for clients. As the client discusses and approves ideas, these sketches and designs are developed further. During the final phases, the architect adds the technical details and specifications to the design.

Once the client approves the design, there's a formal documentation stage where an architect breaks down each stage of the building or project to complete a specialised construction plan. This shows the safe stages of construction and confirms that the building meets quality standards. There may be additional legal requirements put in place on specific sites in the documentation. With documentation completed, architects submit plans for any specific planning permission approvals needed. An architect can support this process by providing additional sketches, plans or engineering details as required.

Requirements To Be An Architect

Architects Typically require an Bachelor's degree in architecture from an accredited school, some after continue to pursue a master's degree in architecture.
All trainee architects are required to take a test known as the Architect Registration Examination, or ARE, after they have the required amount of experience. Most Countries and states have a specific amount of hours of expierience needed before taking the examination.
Passing the ARE leads to certification and licensure. This generally means you can practice in the state where you took the exam, although a national qualification is also offered for those who also intend to work in more than one state.
